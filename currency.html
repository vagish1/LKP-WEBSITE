What Is Currency?

What is currency?

Currency trading, also known as forex trading, involves buying and selling of different currencies in the foreign exchange market. In India, currency trading is regulated by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and is facilitated through currency derivatives traded on stock exchanges.

Currency trading in India allows individuals and businesses to hedge against currency risks and also to speculate on currency price movements. The most commonly traded currency pairs in India include USD/INR, EUR/INR, GBP/INR, and JPY/INR.

To trade in currency derivatives, one needs to open a trading account with a registered broker and deposit a margin amount with the broker. Currency derivatives are traded in lots, and the margin amount required depends on the size of the lot.

Currency trading in India is governed by the rules and regulations of the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) and the RBI. Any violation of these regulations can lead to penalties and legal action.
