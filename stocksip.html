What are Stocks?

Stocks, also known as shares or equities, represent ownership in a publicly-traded company (The Limited Companies). When you buy a stock, you become a shareholder of that company and have a claim on a portion of its assets and earnings.

Companies issue stocks to raise capital from investors, which can be used for various purposes such as expanding the business, paying off debt, or investing in research and development. In return, investors receive a portion of the company's profits in the form of dividends or can sell their shares for a profit if the stock price increases.

Stocks are traded on stock exchanges (NSE and BSE), where buyers and sellers come together to buy and sell shares of publicly- traded companies. The prices of stocks fluctuate based on various factors such as company earnings, market conditions, and investor sentiment

Investing in stocks is considered a popular way to grow one's wealth over the long term. However, it also involves risks, as the prices of stocks can be volatile and can go up or down depending on the market conditions. Therefore, it is important to do thorough research and consult with a financial advisor before making any investment decisions.
